07_classical-auditory-representations_-18-.md 2015-10-08

P19:3-19:5

  • The classification of auditory representations depends on relationship between the signification and the idea being represented, or degree of 'semantic connections to the physical events they represent' (19:3)

  • 'Auditory icons' - very close relationship between the short sound and the idea which it represents

  • Authors use the example of a dot-matrix printer sound to connect to printing, though I wonder how useful that would be today. Thus these connections are also time-dependent.

  • Authors cite research on how long a sound should be in order to be a good icon: 200 to 600 ms
